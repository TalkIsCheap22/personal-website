About Me

I am Yichen Di, an undergraduate student in Software Engineering at Tsinghua University. My primary research interests are in AI for Science, focusing on neural operators and PDE modeling over complex geometries. Previously, I also worked on large language model robustness and watermarking to ensure their safety and reliability.

Currently, I am a research intern at the California Institute of Technology, working with Prof. Anima Anandkumar on neural operator architectures for fluid dynamics, improving their generalizability and scalability. Previously, I worked with Prof. Mingsheng Long on scalable neural PDE solvers, leading to Transolver++ (ICML 2025), and with Prof. Lijie Wen on LLM robustness and watermarking, resulting in WaterSeeker (NAACL 2025) and contributions to the open-source toolkit MarkLLM.

Beyond research, I actively participate in cross-cultural student activities at Tsinghua, including organizing global youth forums and representing my department in music and sports events.

Research

Transolver++: An Accurate Neural Solver for PDEs on Million-Scale Geometries
Yichen Di, Ziqi Wang, Zonghao Chen, Zihao Li, Chen Zhang, Zihan Zhang, Shixuan Sun, Mingsheng Long
[ICML 2025]

Transolver++ is a highly parallel neural solver designed for large-scale PDEs, extending input capacity from millions to tens of millions of mesh points. It effectively captures fine-grained local physics and achieves state-of-the-art performance in industrial-scale simulations.

[Paper] [Code]

WaterSeeker: Pioneering Efficient Detection of Watermarked Segments in Large Documents
Yichen Di, Yuxuan Liu, Ziqi Wang, Siyu Sun, Jiarui Han, Yufei Feng, Lijie Wen
[NAACL 2025, AAAI 2025 PDLM Oral]

WaterSeeker introduces an efficient framework for detecting watermarked text segments within large documents. Combining anomaly extraction with local traversal, it achieves a superior trade-off between detection accuracy and computational efficiency, enhancing the robustness of watermarking detection in real-world scenarios.

[Paper] [Code]

Learning Flow-Physics on Complex Geometries with Neural Operators
Summer Researcher, Anima AI + Science Lab, Caltech

This project develops methods to enhance neural operators for computational fluid dynamics, focusing on both architectural refinements and conditioning strategies. It reveals the importance of dataset completeness and explores applications of neural surrogates to aerodynamic shape optimization.

MarkLLM: An Open-Source Toolkit for LLM Watermarking
Contributor, Tsinghua University
[GitHub, 600+ stars]

MarkLLM is an open-source toolkit offering a unified and extensible framework for LLM watermarking algorithms. It integrates diverse methods within automated pipelines for various watermarking analysis, while also providing visualization tools that make token-level watermarking mechanisms transparent to researchers and practitioners.
